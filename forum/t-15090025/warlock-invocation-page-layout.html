Hey just wanted to double check what we were doing with the warlock page and start a discussion, since I feel its worth discussing how to handle the UA in it?

So recap to bring everyone up to speed, the UA work I was doing previously (I'll get back to double checking it all, sorry, had assignments due in the recent weeks) had us find a bunch of UA invocations that never made it to print, and also a bunch were revised or reworked. Though, for archival purposes I took this as a chance to move all the UA warlock feats into a seperate subpage (linked on the original invocations page), as so that the invocation page only holds printed content, and then if a person wanted to view old UA, they could hop into the subpage. However, a few days back, this was reverted, and the new UA invocations moved onto the main invocation page?

I think since there is uncertainty in what we want to do, I feel bringing it up in a discussion thread is a good idea, so thats what this is. Would we rather all the UA invocations sit on the main invocations page as they did before, or would we rather to have them all on a separate sub page in order to organize them seperately and make it more clear between which is which.

Zkaij 18 Aug 2022 22:57

Hiya. Wasn't aware of this thread, but found it through your previous activity, so here I am to pitch in.

The reason for my (re)revision was firstly because I added (and updated) content which was lost when you reverted: such a reversion should include a port of those changes: in particular, spell formatting, though there might have been other stuff. Secondly, this style matches other places, such as spell list pages (which include UA spells in the tables with the same note), as well as subclasses. However, that latter example gives me an idea that might include the better part of both of our 'versions': we could separate the UA options, listed at the bottom of the page, just how subclasses are separated in that way. That way, all can live on the same page (staying in keeping with other UA-related practices on the wiki), and UA options don't 'get in the way' of the non-UA ones. How does that sound to you?

When I next have the time to do so, I'll check in on the page and work on such an edit (replies on this thread and the state of the page permitting).
